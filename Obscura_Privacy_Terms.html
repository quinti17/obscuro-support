1 · Overview

Obscuro is a macOS utility that redacts faces, licence plates, and other sensitive pixels completely on‑device. We never collect, transmit, or process your images outside your Mac. This document explains your rights, our obligations, and the very limited circumstances under which any data leaves your device (spoiler: it never does).

2 · Privacy Policy

2.1 Data We Do Not Collect

  • No personal data
  • No analytics, crash logs, or usage statistics
  • No identifiers, advertising or otherwise
  • No network calls of any kind (the app is sandboxed without network entitlement)

2.2 Temporary Data We Process Locally

When you open an image Obscuro creates an ephemeral PNG copy in ~/Library/Containers/<bundle-id>/Data/tmp. This file feeds Apple’s Vision/Core Image APIs and is securely deleted once you press Save or Cancel.

2.3 Third‑Party Services

2.5 Security Measures

  • Temporary files are overwritten and deleted via FileManager.removeItem

4 · FAQ

Does Obscuro ever upload my photos?

No. All processing occurs locally; the binary has zero networking capabilities.

Why does the Save panel ask for file‑write permission?

macOS requires the User‑Selected File Read/Write entitlement to present a Save dialog. Obscuro only writes the file you choose.

Can I resize or move manual blur rectangles?

Not yet. Version 1 supports draw‑and‑undo. Resize and move are on the roadmap.

How do I erase every trace of a photo?

Press Cancel or quit the app; temporary files are deleted and working memory is cleared.

I found a bug. How do I report it?

Email info@splizzy.app. Please include steps to reproduce, macOS version, and a sample image if possible.
