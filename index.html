1 · Overview

Obscuro redacts faces, license plates, and other sensitive pixels entirely on your device. No tracking, no uploads, no accounts. Our macOS and iOS apps are sandboxed and process images locally using Apple’s Vision and Core Image frameworks.

2 · Platforms & Features

  • macOS (13+): automatic face detection, manual rectangles, export as PNG or JPEG, and optional Add to Photos.
  • iOS (15+): automatic face detection with simulator-friendly fallback, manual rectangles in an Edit screen, export as PNG or JPEG, and Add to Photos.

All processing is on-device. Temporary files (if any) are stored in the app’s sandboxed tmp directory and removed when you save or cancel.

4 · iOS Permissions

Obscuro requests Photos (Add Only) permission only when you choose Add to Photos:

  • NSPhotoLibraryAddUsageDescription — to save redacted images to your Photos library.

We do not read/browse your library. If you deny permission, you can still export to Files and share normally.

6 · FAQ

Does Obscuro ever upload my photos?

No. All processing is local; the app has no networking capability.

Why does iOS request Photos permission?

Only to save into your library when you choose Add to Photos. It’s optional; you can export to Files without granting access.

Can I export JPEG instead of PNG?

Yes. Both macOS and iOS support export as PNG or JPEG. PNG is lossless; JPEG is smaller.

What if face detection misses someone?

Use the Edit screen to draw additional rectangles manually, then save.

How do I remove every trace?

Press Cancel or quit the app; temporary files and in‑memory images are removed.
